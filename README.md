# PETYA CONSTRUCTED LANGUAGE DOCKET (CONLANG)

**Designation:** Rugged, ancient, mathematical, and martial conlang.
**System Theme:** Slate, Iron, Hearth, and Legacy.

---

## 1. PHONOLOGY (SOUND SYSTEM)
*   **The Rolled R:** Every 'r' in Petya must be heavily trilled/rolled (alveolar trill). Examples: Vrak is pronounced [Vr-r-rahk], Skar is pronounced [Skahr-r-r]. A prolonged final trill indicates emphasis or ceremonial weight.
*   **The Throat Snap (Guttural Clusters):** Letter combinations starting with consonants like Kt- (e.g., Ktag) require a sharp, sudden air release at the back of the throat.
*   **Final Sound Clipping:** Suffixes ending in -ug, -ag, or -oft must be clipped sharply.

## 3. GRAMMAR RULES & STRUCTURES
*   **Rule 1 (Syntax):** Subject + Object + Verb (SOV). Action verb ends the sentence.
*   **Rule 2 (Plural):** Append -yek to the end of the root.
*   **Rule 3 (Past Tense):**
    *   *Common Dialect:* Shift vowel to "u" and append -kd or -gd.
    *   *Formal Dialect:* Shift noun root vowel to "u" and append -d / -td.
*   **Rule 4 (Material Prepend):** Prepend material root to structural nouns (e.g., Fenikt- + Buktstrig).
*   **Rule 5 (Articles):** Use Blik (consonants) or Blikna (vowels).
*   **Rule 6 (Connectors):** Vist (with), Gyat (to), Likst (like), Trog (but), Nagcerkt (if).

---

## 4. MATHEMATICS (BASE-10)
*   **Digits:** 1 (ba), 2 (ya), 3 (na), 4 (fa), 5 (ma), 6 (ra), 7 (sa), 8 (ka), 9 (ta).
*   **Multipliers:** Append -ya to multiply by ten (e.g., 50 = maya).
*   **Intermediate:** Add the digit base directly (e.g., 13 = bayana).
*   **Multiplication:** Use connector dì for higher order (e.g., 100 = bayadìbaya).
